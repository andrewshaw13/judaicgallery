Judaic Gallery

Sabbath and Festival Candlesticks

Shabbat begins every Friday night, and it is tradition to light Sabbath candles at this time. The candles are lit right before sundown and burn while a blessing is said aloud. It is common to use white candles and a beautiful pair of candle sticks when lighting Shabbat candles.

Torah Case and Pointer

During service, a Torah scroll will be taken out of the case and a portion will be read aloud. The reading of the Torah is broken into 54 portions read weekly during Sabbath services. A Torah scroll is hand-written by a specially trained person.

Vessel for the Ritual Washing of Hands

It is tradition for Jews to wash their hands and say a blessing before eating. The ritual is usually done using a two-handled cup, and the water is poured over each hand separately. A blessing is said after the hands are cleaned.
